💡 Advanced Punctuation Examples

Example 1: Oxford Comma

Correct: "I bought apples, oranges, and bananas." 🍏🍊🍌

Incorrect: "I bought apples, oranges and bananas." 🚫

Example 2: Semicolon Usage

Correct: "She loves reading; it's her favorite hobby." 📖

Incorrect: "She loves reading, it's her favorite hobby." 🚫

Example 3: Colon Usage

Correct: "There are three things I enjoy: reading, writing, and traveling." ✈️📚✍️

Incorrect: "There are three things I enjoy, reading, writing, and traveling." 🚫

Example 4: Parentheses

Correct: "She went to the park (her favorite place) every weekend." 🏞️

Incorrect: "She went to the park her favorite place every weekend." 🚫

Example 5: Ellipsis

Correct: "He wanted to say more... but he couldn't find the words." 🤐

Incorrect: "He wanted to say more but he couldn't find the words." 🚫

Example 6: Dash

Correct: "I have a great idea—let's go to the beach!" 🏖️

Incorrect: "I have a great idea, let's go to the beach!" 🚫

Example 7: Quotation Marks

Correct: She said, "I love this book!" 📚

Incorrect: She said I love this book! 🚫

Example 8: Apostrophe

Correct: "It's my favorite day of the week!" 📅

Incorrect: "Its my favorite day of the week!" 🚫

Example 9: Question Mark

Correct: "What time is it?" ⏰

Incorrect: "What time is it." 🚫

Example 10: Exclamation Mark

Correct: "Wow! That was an amazing performance!" 🎭

Incorrect: "Wow. That was an amazing performance." 🚫

Example 11: Comma

Correct: "I want to visit New York, Paris, and Tokyo." 🌆

Incorrect: "I want to visit New York Paris and Tokyo." 🚫

Example 12: Hyphen

Correct: "She is a well-known author." 🖋️

Incorrect: "She is a well known author." 🚫

Example 13: Long Dash

Correct: "The decision was made—no further discussion was needed." 🧐

Incorrect: "The decision was made, no further discussion was needed." 🚫

Example 14: Ellipsis in Dialogue

Correct: "I don't know... maybe we should ask for help." 🤷‍♀️

Incorrect: "I don't know maybe we should ask for help." 🚫

Example 15: Em Dash for Interruptions

Correct: "I was thinking—wait, did you hear that noise?" 🔊

Incorrect: "I was thinking wait, did you hear that noise?" 🚫

Example 16: Quotation Marks for Titles

Correct: "The novel 'Moby Dick' is a classic." 📖

Incorrect: The novel Moby Dick is a classic. 🚫

Example 17: Apostrophe for Possession

Correct: "That is Sarah's book." 📚

Incorrect: "That is Sarah book." 🚫

Example 18: Colon for Explanation

Correct: "She only wanted one thing: peace of mind." 🧘‍♀️

Incorrect: "She only wanted one thing, peace of mind." 🚫

Example 19: Quotation Marks for Direct Speech

Correct: "I will go," he said. 🗣️

Incorrect: I will go, he said. 🚫

Example 20: Comma for Direct Address

Correct: "John, could you please pass me the salt?" 🧂

Incorrect: "John could you please pass me the salt?" 🚫
